Wellness Tips for Students

Practical advice to keep your mind and body in good shape

🧘
Mental Health

It's okay to not be okay

Struggling academically or emotionally doesn't make you weak. Acknowledging how you feel is the first and most important step to getting better.

😴
Physical

Protect your sleep

Sleep is when your brain processes everything you learned. 7-8 hours of quality sleep improves memory, mood and immune function dramatically.

📵
Stress Relief

Take a social media break

Comparing yourself to others online is one of the biggest triggers of anxiety in students. Try a 24-hour social media break — you'll be surprised how peaceful it feels.

🚶
Physical

Move your body daily

Even a 15-minute walk around campus releases endorphins that reduce stress and boost your mood. You don't need a gym — just move.

🗣️
Social

Talk to someone you trust

Bottling up your feelings increases anxiety and depression. Talk to a friend, family member, or counsellor. Saying things out loud reduces their power over you.

📝
Mental Health

Journal your thoughts

Writing down your thoughts for 5-10 minutes before bed helps you process emotions, reduces anxiety and gives you clarity on what's actually bothering you.

⏸️
Stress Relief

Learn to pause before reacting

When you feel overwhelmed, pause. Take 3 deep breaths before responding to a situation. This one habit prevents most unnecessary conflicts and regrettable decisions.

🎯
Mental Health

Set small, achievable daily goals

Instead of thinking about everything at once, write down just 3 things to accomplish today. Completing small goals builds confidence and reduces overwhelm.

🤲
Social

Ask for help — it's strength not weakness

Whether it's academics, finances or mental health — asking for help is one of the most courageous things a student can do. No one succeeds completely alone.

Support Resources at PCU

You are not alone — here's where to get help

🏥

PCU Campus Clinic

Visit the campus clinic for physical and mental health support. Open on weekdays during school hours.

🧑‍💼

Student Affairs Office

For welfare issues, complaints or guidance. The Student Affairs team is there to support your campus experience.

🙏

Campus Chapel / Fellowship

Spiritual support and community. Many students find strength, peace and belonging through campus fellowships.

👥

NACOS Study Groups

Academic stress is reduced when you're not studying alone. Use GradGuide's Study Group Finder to connect with classmates.

📞

Talk to a Trusted Lecturer

If you're struggling, approach a lecturer you trust. Most are willing to listen and point you in the right direction.

❤️

Your Friends & Family

Sometimes the best support is someone who already knows and loves you. Don't underestimate the power of a phone call home.
